\exercise[]{
\textbf{Conjugate prior for univariate Gaussian in exponential family form.}

Let's start backwards. We are told that the conjugate prior is given by

\begin{align}
    N(\mu|\gamma,\lambda(2\alpha - 1))Ga(\lambda|\alpha,\beta) & \propto
    exp(-\frac{\lambda(2\alpha-1)}{2}(\mu-\gamma)^2)
    \lambda^{\alpha-1}exp(-\lambda\beta) \\
    & = exp(-\frac{\lambda(2\alpha-1)}{2}(\mu-\gamma)^2 - \lambda\beta)
        \lambda^{\alpha-1} \\
    & = exp(-\frac{\lambda}{2}\left [ (2\alpha-1)(\mu-\gamma)^2 -
        2\beta \right ]) \lambda^{\alpha-1}
\end{align}

In the problem, we are asked to parameterize this distribution using $\mu$
and $\lambda = 1/\sigma^2$. Thus, the likelihood is given by

\begin{align}
    p(D|\mu,\lambda) & = \prod_{i=1}^N \frac{\lambda}{\sqrt{2\pi}}
            exp(-\frac{\lambda}{2}(x_i-\mu)^2) \\
    & = \lambda^N (2\pi)^{-N/2} \prod_{i=1}^N
        exp(-\frac{\lambda}{2}(x_i-\mu)^2) \\
    & = \lambda^N (2\pi)^{-N/2} exp(-\frac{\lambda}{2}
        \sum_{i=1}^N (x_i-\mu)^2)
\end{align}

}
